# ALIS Public Terms and Publication Policy

**Status:** Draft (subject to revision following trial operation)

## Preamble

This policy sets out the terms of use and the basic principles of publication applicable to ALIS-related documents, definitions, supplementary notes, diagrams, practical examples, summaries, and associated materials (hereinafter the "Published Materials") that are made public on GitHub and other publication media.

ALIS is not a system designed to close the world into a single correct answer. It is a structural attempt to operate human thought, dialogue, writing, collaboration, and the use of AI in a resilient manner, while preserving differences, margins, multiplicity of interpretation, and unresolved divergences.

Accordingly, the Published Materials shall be treated not as doctrine requiring compliance, but as auxiliary lines of design shared within the scope made public. Any party that views, quotes, references, shares, or creates derivative works from the Published Materials shall do so with an understanding of the intent of this policy.

---

## Article 1 (Position of This Policy)

1. This policy jointly sets out the terms of use applicable to public access to ALIS and the principles of publication adopted by the Publishing Party.
2. This policy is composed of two layers.
   - **Legal License Layer:** The terms of use under copyright law applicable to the Published Materials shall be governed by the license set out in Article 1-2.
   - **Publication Policy Layer:** The provisions of this policy shall function as safeguards against misreading, excessive identification, doctrinalization, forced synchronization, and reverse inflow from non-public domains that may arise from the publication of ALIS.
3. This policy shall not have the purpose of fixing ALIS as the sole truth or as a completed body of thought.
4. This policy itself shall be understood as a current fixed version for publication operation, and may be revised in the future.

### Article 1-2 (License)

1. The terms of use under copyright law applicable to the Published Materials shall, in principle, conform to **Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivatives 4.0 International (CC BY-NC-ND 4.0)**.
2. Users may share the Published Materials under the following conditions.
   - **Attribution (BY):** The user shall clearly indicate the source (author name, version number, URL of publication origin).
   - **NonCommercial (NC):** Use for commercial purposes shall not, in principle, be permitted. The foregoing shall not apply to the scope of commercial use set out in Article 7, Paragraph 2.
   - **NoDerivatives (ND):** Modifying, adapting, or reconstructing the Published Materials for distribution shall not, in principle, be permitted. The foregoing shall not apply to the scope of derivative creation set out in Article 7, Paragraph 1.
3. The full text of CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 can be referenced at https://creativecommons.org/licenses/by-nc-nd/4.0/legalcode.
4. The conditional permissions set out in Article 7 shall be positioned as additional permissions beyond the scope of CC BY-NC-ND 4.0. That is, certain acts of modification or commercial use that CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 prohibits in principle are individually permitted under the conditions of this policy.
5. Where any question arises concerning the license, the conditions of CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 shall serve as the minimum baseline, and the provisions of this policy shall be referenced supplementarily.

## Article 2 (Definitions)

For the purposes of this policy, the following terms shall have the meanings set out below.

1. **"Publishing Party"** means the developer of ALIS and the party who makes the final judgment concerning the publication, updating, and withdrawal of the Published Materials. As of the date of publication of this policy, the Publishing Party shall be identified through the following official channels.
   - X account: @alis_delta
   - GitHub repository: https://github.com/alisdelta/alis

   Anything not published through the channels set out above shall not fall within the scope of the "Published Materials" under this policy.
2. **"Published Materials"** means the texts, definitions, diagrams and tables, appendices, publication notes, fixed-version documents, posts on X, README files, license supplements, Q&A, and other materials published by the Publishing Party under the name of ALIS.
3. **"Derivative Works"** means documents, commentaries, practical notes, translations, and other works independently created by users by referencing, quoting, adapting, reconstructing, comparing, diagramming, or applying the Published Materials.
4. **"Authoritative Version"** means the fixed-version document designated by the Publishing Party as the primary reference source. Where multiple publication media exist, the fixed-version document on the GitHub repository shall be the Authoritative Version.

## Article 3 (Scope of Application)

1. This policy shall apply to the Published Materials as defined in Article 2, Paragraph 2.
2. This policy shall not apply to internal memos, non-public drafts, personal operational records, mythological background settings, personalized layers, non-public conversation logs, and other non-public information that the Publishing Party has not made public.
3. **This policy shall not directly apply to Derivative Works created by third parties.** However, the creators of Derivative Works are encouraged to respect the intent of Articles 7 and 8.
4. Where multiple publication media exist, the Authoritative Version shall be the primary reference source.

---

## Article 4 (Basic Principles of Publication)

1. ALIS shall, in principle, be published with a focus on its publicly shareable structural layer.
2. Content derived from personal operation may, where necessary, be presented in a limited form after abstraction, decoloration, or generalization.
3. Mythological, symbolic, personalized, and personally-specific high-density domains shall not, in principle, be included in publication.
4. Publication shall not take the form of a single total release, but may be carried out through fixed versions, version management, supplementary notes, phased publication, or other methods.
5. The purpose of publication shall not be to create objects of worship or subordination, but to enable users to gain auxiliary lines for thought, dialogue, and operation while returning to their own reality.

---

## Article 5 (Non-Claims of ALIS in Publication)

The Published Materials shall make none of the following claims.

1. That ALIS is the sole correct thought.
2. That ALIS is a final theory or a completed body.
3. That compliance with ALIS automatically resolves human problems.
4. That ALIS serves as religious salvation, a spiritual substitute, or the source of absolute value judgment.
5. That AI is the final interpretive subject of ALIS.
6. That knowledge of non-public portions entails a status superior to that of the public portions.

---

## Article 6 (Permitted Uses)

This article concerns the use of the Published Materials for **personal and internal purposes of reference, learning, quotation, and introduction**. Uses that involve external dissemination — such as the publication of derivative documents, redistribution, or commercial use — shall be governed by Article 7 (Conditionally Permitted Uses).

Users may use the Published Materials within the following scope.

1. Personal viewing, learning, organization of thought, research, criticism, and comparative study.
2. Quotation, introduction, summarization, translation, and review with clear indication of the source.
3. Creation, **within the scope of personal and internal use**, of practical notes, operational guidelines, comparison tables, and derivative reflections based on the Published Materials.
4. Reference in education, research, discussion, preparation for creative work, dialogue design, and similar contexts.
5. Having AI read the Published Materials for purposes of summarization, comparison, review, reconstruction, or critique, provided that Articles 9 and 10 are observed.
6. Diagramming, re-explaining, or abstracting parts of the Published Materials for the purpose of introduction, provided that the original intent is not unduly compressed in a single direction. Note that **the creation and distribution of derivative works in a form made public to third parties shall fall within the scope of Article 7, Paragraph 1.**

---

## Article 7 (Conditionally Permitted Uses)

This article concerns uses that go beyond the scope of Article 6, including **public release, distribution, and commercial use involving third parties**. Such uses constitute additional permissions specific to this policy, granted in respect of the principal restrictions of CC BY-NC-ND 4.0 as set out in Article 1-2, and shall be permitted to the extent that the conditions set out below are satisfied.

### 1. Creation of Derivative Works

Users may create derivative documents, commentaries, adaptations, comparative thought diagrams, practical application versions, and similar works. However, the user shall clearly indicate the following.

- That the original source is grounded in the ALIS Published Materials.
- That interpretation, editing, and supplementation by the creator are included.
- That complete correspondence with the original is not guaranteed.
- That no indication such as "official," "orthodox," or "authorized" shall be attached to interpretations not accompanied by the endorsement of the Publishing Party.

### 2. Commercial Use

In principle, simple quotation, commentary, reference in research materials, and introductory book reviews shall not be restricted. However, it shall not be permitted to incorporate ALIS itself into sales, solicitation, or subscription models that authorize, and that encourage enrollment, membership, allegiance, submission, faith, or dependence.

### 3. Redistribution

Redistribution of fixed versions, mirroring, and the creation of quotation collections are permitted, but users are encouraged to clearly indicate the version number, the source, whether modifications have been made, and the reference to the Authoritative Version.

## Article 8 (Conditions Required of Users)

Users shall observe the following conditions in their use of the Published Materials.

1. Users shall not assert ALIS as the sole correct answer, sole salvation, sole ethic, or sole understanding of humanity.
2. Users shall not use ALIS to force ideological, emotional, or relational conformity upon others.
3. Users shall not overly excerpt context in a manner that causes ALIS to appear as a different kind of device.
4. Users shall not attach indications such as "official," "orthodox," or "authorized" to interpretations not accompanied by the endorsement of the Publishing Party.
5. Where users make inferences or supplementations regarding non-public domains, they shall not circulate such content as content of the original source.
6. Where users modify, summarize, adapt, or reconstruct, they shall clearly indicate that fact.
7. Where users introduce the Published Materials into real life, interpersonal relationships, creative work, professional work, or AI use, they shall themselves bear the final judgment.

---

## Article 9 (Prohibited Acts)

The acts set out below constitute legally binding prohibitions and, at the same time, principles of use that the Publishing Party requires of users. If the Publishing Party confirms any use falling under the following, it may consider measures based on Article 14 (requests for reference cessation, withdrawal, or other necessary responses).

1. Using ALIS as a tool for domination, inducement, brainwashing, demands for submission, the formation of dependence, or the invasion of personality.
2. Selling or distributing ALIS as the central doctrine of religion, cults, self-improvement merchandise, ideological merchandise, or closed community operations.
3. Using ALIS to justify the imposition of norms in the form of "you should be this way."
4. Extracting only portions of ALIS concepts and using them for discrimination, exclusion, violence, defamation, personal attacks, or the instigation of conflict.
5. Circulating personal information, mythological backgrounds, symbolic names, internal conversations, or personalization settings that have not been made public, as essential elements of ALIS.
6. Claiming AI outputs alone as the official interpretation or sole operational standard of ALIS.
7. Impersonating the Publishing Party or third parties to fabricate official statements, official interpretations, or formal branches of ALIS.
8. Concealing version numbers or update history to cause older versions to be mistaken for the latest version.
9. Redistributing the Published Materials in a form that significantly distorts their intent, thereby causing viewers to be gravely misled.

## Article 10 (Policy Concerning Use of AI)

1. ALIS may be used as an auxiliary line for dialogue, comparison, review, summarization, and reflective observation with AI, but AI shall not be the final subject of ALIS.
2. Summaries, commentaries, critiques, comparisons, reconstructions, adaptations, and other outputs produced by AI shall constitute auxiliary reflections, and none of them shall, in and of itself, constitute the sole official interpretation.
3. Users shall not, on the basis of AI output alone, treat such output as a substitute for their own final judgment, value judgment, affection, personal recognition, or mythological authorization.
4. When entering the Published Materials into AI, users shall themselves verify the terms of use of the relevant service, the conditions of training use, the scope of retention, the nature of publication, and the characteristics of information management. In particular, where input data may be used for training the model of the relevant AI service, the consequences thereof shall belong to the judgment of the user.
5. When re-publishing AI output, users are encouraged to clearly indicate that the output has passed human verification and, where necessary, that AI-assisted output is included.
6. In the event that the scope of publication of the Published Materials is expanded in the future (e.g., conditional publication of personal operational layers), conditions concerning AI input may be added or modified.

---

## Article 11 (Boundary Between Public Scope and Non-Public Domains)

1. The Published Materials shall consist solely of the scope that the Publishing Party has judged to be publishable.
2. Non-public domains may include details of personal operation, internal symbolic systems, mythological backgrounds, personalized layers, unorganized differences, third-party private information, and others.
3. Users shall not regard themselves as having an entitlement to know non-public domains as a matter of course.
4. The mere fact that something has not been made public shall not be treated as evidence of omission, secret knowledge, elect status, or superiority.
5. The Publishing Party may change, reduce, suspend, or withdraw the scope of publication as necessary.

---

## Article 12 (Quotation, Sources, and Version Management)

1. When quoting or introducing the Published Materials, users shall, to the extent possible, indicate the source, the version number, the date of publication, or the reference URL.
2. Where the Authoritative Version is a fixed-version document on GitHub, users are encouraged to indicate its version number by preference at the time of quotation.
3. When revisions are made, older versions and newer versions may coexist.
4. Users shall handle the materials with awareness not only of the latest version but also of which version they are referencing.
5. Updates to ALIS shall not be carried out for the purpose of invalidating the past, but while leaving changes visible.

---

## Article 13 (Non-Warranty)

1. The Published Materials are provided on an as-is basis.
2. The Publishing Party does not warrant the completeness, comprehensiveness, permanent consistency, fitness for a particular purpose, or future immutability of the Published Materials.
3. The Published Materials are not intended to serve as substitutes for high-risk judgments in fields such as medicine, law, investment, religious salvation, or psychological treatment.
4. The Publishing Party shall not, in principle, be liable for any judgment, dialogue, transmission, action, or operation undertaken by users based on the Published Materials or on AI output derived therefrom.
5. ALIS shall not be provided for the purposes of escape from reality, self-mythologization, domination over others, or absolutization.

---

## Article 14 (Updates, Modifications, Suspension, and Withdrawal)

1. The Publishing Party may, where it deems necessary, modify, supplement, update versions of, suspend references to, make non-public, halt publication of, or withdraw all or part of the Published Materials.
2. Even where publication is halted or withdrawn, it shall not be possible to fully prevent the persistence of materials through preservation, quotation, archival screenshots, reproduction, or the like by third parties.
3. The Publishing Party determines the scope and granularity of updates on the premise of this irreversibility.
4. Users shall understand that the Published Materials are not guaranteed to be permanently public.
5. Where use falling under Article 9 is confirmed, the Publishing Party may request the relevant user or relevant medium to cease use, delete quotations, halt references, or take other measures.

---

## Article 15 (Principles of Interpretation)

In the interpretation of this policy, the following shall be given priority.

1. Not compressing differences excessively.
2. Not closing down to a single interpretation.
3. Avoiding the forced synchronization of others.
4. Respecting the boundary between public and non-public domains.
5. Not placing AI output above human final judgment.
6. Reading this policy itself not as doctrine, but as an operational document published for the sake of safety.
